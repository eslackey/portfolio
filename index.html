recent work

Seamless Service Station

The Seamless Service Station test replaced red price scanners in stores with a mounted iPad. The iPad enables self-service features like search, weekly ad, store maps, and more. I created initial prototypes and visual design that went to production. The test has been expanded to a 70+ store pilot.

Request a Team Member

Request a Team Member was a test that allowed shoppers to request assistance in-store with their iPhones using the Target app. I designed the employee-facing app, which utilized beacon technology to locate guests and manage requests. The concept is planned to roll-out chain wide.

myDevice App Consolidation

Store PDAs had over 60 task related applications. I designed a consolidated app concept that combined all of the most frequent tasks for team members into a single iPod touch app. The concept was the foundation of a large initiative that released iPod touch devices to the entire chain.

Team Member Recognition

This proof of concept allowed team members in stores to give recognition to each other using an iPad. The iPad was docked in the break room and replaced paper recognition cards that are frequently exchanged in stores. I designed the user interface of the iPad application that was tested in stores.

Gamification

Gamification was a proof of concept that tested the motivational impact of rewarding in-store employees with points, badges, and digital recognition for their work. I designed several game concepts for in-store tasks, the user interface for the application, and a library of badges for the test.

Mobile LOD

The Leader on Duty (LOD) is in charge of nearly everything that happens in a store. This proof of concept provided all of the data an LOD needs throughout the day, right at their finger tips. I designed early prototypes and iterated the design to ensure we provided all the most critical data at a glance.

Compare and Attach

This application concept was created to give team members access to better sales tools. It gives the ability to compare products and recommend attachment items. I designed the user interface for the concept and it has been approved to move forward as a chain-wide roll out on team member devices.

Ship from Store Dashboard

I consulted on a project that needed to create an easily consumable, mobile dashboard to track shipments from stores. The process was originally completed using an excel spreadsheet. I converted the spreadsheet data into a simple, visually appealing dashboard. The concept was designed for both iPod Touch and iPad.

Emergency Flipchart Mobile

Stores need to be prepared for anything. Previously, there were numerous packets, checklists, and guides for emergencies. This app consolidates all of the emergency procedures into a single app. It also syncs emergency phone numbers for all devices in-store. I created the initial prototype design.

Checklane Survey

Store leaders always want feedback on service. Target traditionally uses a survey link printed on receipts for feedback. This test was designed to get quick, one-quesiton feedback from shoppers. Questions were displayed on an iPod Touch mounted in checklanes. I designed the user interface, which included several layouts for all of the different types of questions.

Leader Task Management

Stores can be assigned tasks from numerous areas of the business. Leaders at the regional and district level wanted the ability to quickly see the status of assigned tasks in each of their stores. I designed an app that accessed data from the store task management app, and provides a high level view of all active, in progress, not started, and overdue tasks in stores.

Point of Sale

One of the most important components of retail is the checkout experience. I had the opportunity to redesign Point of Sale for Target stores. I faced constraints of legacy hardware, keyboard-only input, performance, and numerous edge-case scenarios. I worked closely with the point of sale experts to create the new design concept.
